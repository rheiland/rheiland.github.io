Sculpture, Geometry and Computer Science

Randy Heiland, Charles O. Perry, Barbara Ream, Andrew Lumsdaine
(accepted for a lecture presentation at SIAM CSE 2011)

The outline of this project is as follows:

  1. Introduce students to a particular work of art, the Indiana Arc sculpture (by Charles Perry), located on the IU-Bloomington campus.
    • Field trip to the campus.
    • Let them observe it, sketch it (don't allow photos?), make written notes.
    • Let them briefly visit the adjoining IU Art Museum (there's a reason).
    • Brief visit to IU's Data Center (home to supercomputers, etc)
  2. Take a brief online ("before") survey about that experience.
  3. Classroom: Share their thoughts - partly from the survey.
    • Did they like the sculpture? Why/why not?
    • Begin the discussion on possible ways to contruct it.
    • Manipulatives.
  4. Classroom: Make connections to algebra and geometry
  5. Classroom: Modeling software (help install on their laptops)
  6. Classroom: Construct a 3D model of the sculpture on the computer - visualize.
  7. Classroom: Analyze their model.
  8. Classroom: Metaphors for the sculpture?
  9. Take a brief online ("after") survey about the overall experience.
  10. Present results at the SIAM conference Feb 28.
